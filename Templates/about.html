In the current era, Heart Failure is one of the common diseases that can lead to dangerous situations. Every year almost 26 million patients are affected by this kind of disease. It is a serious condition with high prevalence (about 2% in the adult population in developed countries, and more than 8% in patients older than 75 years). About 3–5% of hospital admissions are linked with heart failure incidents. Heart failure is the first cause of admission by healthcare professionals in their clinical practice. The costs are very high, reaching up to 2% of the total health costs in developed countries. From the heart consultant and surgeon’s point of view, it is complex to predict heart failure at the right time. Building an effective disease management strategy requires analysis of a large amount of data, early detection of the disease, assessment of the severity, and early prediction of adverse events. This will inhibit the progression of the disease, will improve the quality of life of the patients, and will reduce the associated medical costs. Fortunately, classification and predicting models are there, which can aid the medical field and can illustrate how to use the medical data in an efficient way. For classification and efficient prediction, machine learning techniques have been employed. This system aims to help individuals predict if there are possibilities of heart diseases and provide further steps to take care of their heart health.
